About Spine Gourd

Spine gourd, scientifically known as Momordica dioica, is a unique species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes gourds and squashes. It is a perennial, dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants. The spine gourd is characterized by its climbing nature, often reaching lengths of 2-3 meters. It is propagated through underground tubers, which allows for easy cultivation even in less fertile soils.

The plant’s stem is slender, branched, and furrowed, with a shiny appearance. Its tendrils, which aid in climbing, are elongated and simple. The leaves of the spine gourd are small and membranous, varying in size and shape but generally having a broad ovate outline with deep lobes. The flowers are small and yellow, leading to the development of its distinctive fruits. These fruits are small, dark green, and either round or oval, covered with soft spines, giving the plant its common name.

Spine gourd is native to tropical regions of Asia, with a widespread distribution across India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, China, Japan, Southeast Asia, Polynesia, as well as tropical Africa and South America. It holds commercial importance and is used as a vegetable throughout India and parts of South Asia. The fruits are typically cooked with spices or fried and can be paired with meat or fish to create flavorful dishes.

In addition to its culinary uses, the spine gourd plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unusual fruits. It can be grown in home gardens and farms alike, providing both aesthetic appeal and a source of food. The spine gourd is a testament to the diversity and utility of plants within the Cucurbitaceae family.

Detailed Description of cooking process



Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds Spine Gourd
  • 2 tablespoons sunflower oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chillies, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped


Cooking Process:


  1. Heat the sunflower oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and chopped green chillies. Sauté until the seeds start to splutter.
  2. Add the chopped onions, minced garlic, and ginger to the pan. Cook until the onions are translucent.
  3. Stir in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and tamarind paste to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  5. Wash the spine gourd thoroughly and slice them into halves. Add the spine gourd to the pan and mix well.


Final preparation:
  • Pour in the coconut milk and add salt to taste. Cover and simmer until the spine gourd is cooked and the curry reaches the desired consistency.
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves before serving.
