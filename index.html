About Forgotton
Vegetables

Discover the world of lesser-known vegetables, once a staple in diets of the past. These veggies are packed with nutrients and offer more health benefits than many common ones we eat today. They’re not just food; they’re a piece of our history, full of unique tastes and health advantages. When you choose these old-fashioned veggies, you’re not just making your meals more interesting—you’re also helping to keep the earth’s plant variety alive. Let’s bring back these nutritious veggies and enjoy flavors that have been cherished for generations.

Elephant Yam

Elephant yam, scientifically known as Amorphophallus paeoniifolius, is a tropical tuber crop that is native to Island Southeast Asia1. It is widely cultivated for its edible tubers in various parts of Asia, as well as in Madagascar

Red Okra

Red okra, known for its striking color, is a variety of the traditional green okra and belongs to the mallow family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and hollyhock1. Native to Ethiopia, okra is the only member of this family to bear edible fruit.

Lotus Stem

The lotus stem is a part of the lotus plant, which is also known as Nelumbo nucifera. It’s a long and crunchy stem that grows underwater and is used in many dishes in Asian countries. The lotus plant is very important in countries like India

Sun Berry

The sunberry, also known as wonderberry or scientifically as Solanum retroflexum, is a plant with a rich history and intriguing characteristics. Developed in the early 1900s by the famed horticulturist Luther Burbank.

Clove Beans

Clove beans, also known as Ipomoea muricata, are a vining plant that bears clusters of fruit. Despite their name, they are not actually beans but are more closely related to the Morning Glory family.They are popular in Kerala.

Palmyra Sprout

Palmyra sprouts, also known as Panam Kizhangu in Tamil, are derived from the Palmyra palm tree, scientifically referred to as Borassus Flabellifer. These trees are similar in appearance to coconut trees but are shorter.

Spine Gourd

Spine gourd, scientifically known as Momordica dioica, is a unique species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes gourds and squashes. It is a perennial, dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants.
