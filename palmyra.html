About Palmyra Sprouts

Palmyra sprouts, also known as Panam Kizhangu in Tamil, are derived from the Palmyra palm tree, scientifically referred to as Borassus Flabellifer. These trees are similar in appearance to coconut trees but are shorter and have fan-shaped leaves with pointed tips. The Palmyra palm is a versatile plant, often called a “Kalpavriksha” or “Miracle Tree” because all its parts are utilized for various purposes.

The sprouts are one of the oldest fibers used in Southern Indian cuisine and are enjoyed for their unique taste and texture. They can be consumed raw, roasted, or boiled with a pinch of salt. Cultivation involves planting the palm seeds close together in a shallow trench, which is regularly watered.

The sprouts germinate within a month and are then harvested by removing the outer cover after separating the root and tip. Found primarily in Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Orissa, and some parts of Palghar, the Palmyra sprout is a traditional delicacy with a woody taste and fibrous texture, often enjoyed freshly boiled.

The Palmyra palm, from which the sprouts are harvested, is deeply ingrained in the cultural and economic fabric of the regions where it grows. In Tamil Nadu, for instance, the tree is not just a source of food but also a symbol of prosperity and an integral part of the local ecosystem. The leaves of the Palmyra palm are used for thatching roofs and making mats, baskets, and fans, showcasing the tree’s versatility beyond its edible offerings. The Palmyra palm has a slow growth rate, taking about 15 to 20 years to produce its first inflorescence flowers, which is when the sex of the plant can be determined.

Moreover, the Palmyra palm plays a significant role in local traditions and festivals. The sprouts, which are available primarily from January to March, are often associated with Pongal, a harvest festival celebrated in Tamil Nadu. During this time, the sprouts are a common sight in markets and are considered a seasonal delicacy. The cultivation of Palmyra sprouts is a testament to the sustainable agricultural practices in the region, as the trees require minimal maintenance once established and can thrive in the local climate.

Detailed Description of Cooking Palmyra Sprouts


Ingredients


  • Steamed / Boiled Palmyra Sprouts: 6-8 nos.
  • Coconut (sliced): a handful (or more according to your wish)
  • Palm Sugar: 4-5 tablespoons
  • Finely chopped Nuts: 3 tablespoons (Cashew, Almond, Walnuts etc.)
  • Cardamom powder: a pinch
  • Ghee (Clarified butter): ½ teaspoon


Instructions


  • Preparation:

      1. Peel the outer skin of the Palmyra sprouts.

      2. Cut them into half vertically to remove the inner portion which is like a stick.

      3. Peel off the extra fibres and slice them into small pieces.

      4. Blend the palm sprout slices and coconut in a food processor to get a smooth powdery consistency (not too smooth like a paste).

      5. Add in the blended mixture, palm sugar, finely chopped nuts, and cardamom powder in a mixing bowl and mix them well.

      6. Now apply some ghee (Clarified butter) / Butter in your hands and take a spoonful of mixture and make it into a ball (laddoo).




Final Preparation:
  • Do the same with the remaining mixture.
  • Serve with garnished nuts.
